Why invest?

You probably know that the aim of investing is to make money. However, most people underestimate the benefit of long term investing.

This partially due to the unintuitive nature of compound interest. For example, if you invest $1000 at 10% interest a year, after the first year you will receive $100, giving you a total of $1100. The next year, you earn 10% on $1100, which is $110.

You might think this difference isn't much - only $10 more in the second year. And you are right. But if we kept this investment over a whole career...

In the 40th year, your investment would be worth... $45,000. And that's just from $1000 dollars once off this year. If you instead decided to add $1000 of your own money each year, you would have over $485,000. That's only from a $40,000 total outlay!
