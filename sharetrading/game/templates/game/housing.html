Housing Market

You can also invest in the housing market. Unlike a bank account, the return on a housing investment is variable - you are not sure how much it will be when you buy. Housing prices can go up and down.

The return from a housing investment comes from a combination of rent, and the increase in the value of the property. However, there is always a chance that you can't find a tenant, or that the value of the property falls.

On average, the return from a housing investment will be higher than the return from a bank account. If it wasn't, why would you ever invest in property? It would be better to take the safe option of a bank account.

Things to be aware of in property investments include:

  • There are usually large taxes on purchasing property (e.g. stamp duty), which means you need more return before you break even.
  • Missing even a few weeks of rent because you cannot find a tennant is very expensive. You could be better off reducing your rent by 5% rather than missing 3 weeks of rent.
  • Different properties will perform differently based on many different factors - just because the 'property market' returned 10%, doesn't mean every property did!

Reasons you might consider investing in property include:

  • You need somewhere for you and your family to live, and you don't want to rent.
  • You want to be protected against property price rises, so you don't get priced out of the market
  • You want to take advantage of a leveraged investment strategy - no bank will let you borrow $500k to invest in shares!

Reasons you might consider a different investment instead include:

  • The risk is too high, and you want to be more certain of your investment returns. A savings account may be a better option in this case.
  • You may need access to your cash in the near future. It can take a long time to sell property, which might leave you with no money for other things.
  • You are only investing for a shorter-term period. Buying and selling property involves a lot of taxes and agent fees, and so it is best held as a long term investment.
  • You are willing to bear even greater risk for the chance at higher returns (see the Stockmarket section).
